title: 'How would you explain the LNG commodity market from a business and data perspective?'
question: 'In an interview, how would you explain what LNG is, why it matters commercially, and what kind of data is important in LNG trading or operations?'
answer: >-
  I would explain LNG as liquefied natural gas, which is natural gas cooled to around minus 162 degrees Celsius so it can be transported efficiently by ship and stored before being regasified for use.
  
  - **Why It Matters Commercially**: LNG connects gas producers and importing markets globally, which makes it important for energy security, flexible supply and price balancing across regions.
  - **Business Drivers**: The market is influenced by supply and demand, weather, storage levels, shipping availability, contract structures, geopolitics and regional benchmark prices.
  - **Operational View**: LNG value chains involve production, liquefaction, shipping, terminals, regasification and final delivery to utilities or industrial customers.
  - **Data That Matters**: Useful data includes cargo schedules, vessel tracking, terminal throughput, storage levels, contract terms, spot and forward prices, demand forecasts and operational incidents.
  - **Why This Matters for Data Work**: From a data perspective, the goal is to turn fragmented operational and market data into reliable reporting and decision support for forecasting, risk management and performance monitoring.
  
  A strong interview answer is that LNG is both a physical commodity and a data-intensive business domain, where timely and trusted information can directly improve commercial and operational decisions.
