title: 'How do you demonstrate learning agility and technical curiosity?'
question: 'How would you show your willingness to learn new technologies, develop technical skills and apply knowledge in practice?'
answer: >-
  I would show this through how I learn and how quickly I turn learning into usable output.
  
  - **Structured Learning**: When I meet a new tool, I start with core concepts, then build a small practical example so I understand both theory and usage.
  - **Ask Good Questions**: I try to ask precise questions that unblock progress rather than waiting passively for answers.
  - **Apply Learning Quickly**: I retain knowledge better when I use it straight away, for example by building a notebook, testing a transformation or documenting a pattern.
  - **Seek Feedback Early**: I would rather have my approach corrected early by a senior engineer than spend too long reinforcing the wrong pattern.
  - **Reflect and Improve**: After finishing a task, I think about what I learned, what confused me and what I should practice next.
  
  For this apprenticeship, I would connect learning agility directly to the role: Peabody is offering structured mentoring, code reviews and real project work, and that is exactly the environment where I can learn quickly and contribute steadily.
