title: 'How do you collaborate with stakeholders and work well in an agile team?'
question: 'How do you work with others to understand requirements, share knowledge and contribute to team objectives?'
answer: >-
  I try to make collaboration practical, clear and dependable.
  
  - **Understand the Real Need**: I start by clarifying the business goal, the data needed and how the output will be used, rather than just taking a request at face value.
  - **Communicate Progress Clearly**: I keep people updated on what I am doing, what is blocked and when they can expect the next step.
  - **Work Well with Different Roles**: In a data team that means collaborating with engineers, analysts, product owners and business stakeholders, each of whom views the problem differently.
  - **Contribute in Agile Ceremonies**: Stand-ups, sprint planning and retrospectives are useful because they surface blockers early and help the team improve how it works.
  - **Share Knowledge**: I document decisions, explain my reasoning and help others where I can, because strong teams are easier to scale than isolated individuals.
  - **Stay Open to Feedback**: Good collaboration includes being able to adjust your approach when someone has better context or a better idea.
  
  For Peabody specifically, I would connect this to working with product owners and analysts to turn operational needs into trusted data products that support decision-making across the organisation.
